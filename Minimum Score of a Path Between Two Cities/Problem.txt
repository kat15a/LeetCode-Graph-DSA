Problem: Minimum Score of a Path Between Two Cities

Problem Statement:
You are given n cities connected by bidirectional roads.
Each road is represented as [u, v, distance].

The score of a path is defined as the minimum edge distance along that path.

Task:
Return the minimum possible score of a path between city 1 and city n.

Note:
- The graph may not be fully connected.
- A path may revisit cities and roads multiple times.
- It is guaranteed that at least one path exists between city 1 and city n.

Approach:
- Build the graph as an adjacency list.
- Since revisiting roads is allowed, every city in the connected component
  containing city 1 is reachable.
- Perform DFS/BFS starting from city 1.
- While traversing, keep updating the answer with the minimum road distance
  encountered.
- The minimum edge in the connected component containing city 1 is the answer,
  because we can always traverse through that edge and still reach city n.

Algorithm Used:
- Depth First Search (DFS)
  or
- Breadth First Search (BFS)

Time Complexity:
- O(V + E)

Space Complexity:
- O(V + E)

Interview Insight:
- The key observation is that paths are allowed to revisit cities and roads.
- Therefore, we only need to explore the connected component containing city 1.
- The answer is simply the minimum edge weight present in that connected component.
- Many candidates incorrectly try to use Dijkstra's algorithm, but shortest path algorithms are unnecessary here.
